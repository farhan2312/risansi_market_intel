[P]1. Executive Summary
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]Risansi&apos;s Sales &amp; Marketing team currently manages its entire commercial operation — client master data, visit records, revenue history, equipment assessments, and competitive intelligence — through a single shared Excel workbook containing over 3,500 client records across 14 inter-linked sheets. This file is updated manually by multiple team members, shared via email, and queried by management for reporting.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]This approach creates structural risks that limit the team&apos;s ability to operate at scale: data conflicts when multiple users edit simultaneously, version proliferation across email threads, no real-time visibility for management, no field-capture capability for reps on-site at client locations, and no systematic intelligence on competitor installed base.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]This document specifies the requirements for the Risansi Sales &amp; Marketing Intelligence Platform — a purpose-built web and mobile application that replaces all Excel- and email-based processes with a structured, real-time, field-ready digital system.
[P]
[P]
[P]Scope of Digitization
[P]Client master data management (3,500+ records, growing)
[P]Field visit planning, scheduling, and GPS-verified check-in
[P]On-site data capture: visit reports, equipment assessments, contact management
[P]Revenue tracking: year-on-year turnover by pump and spare category
[P]Competitive intelligence: installed base per client, market share analytics
[P]Pipeline and forecast management
[P]Role-based dashboards: rep, zonal manager, national head
[P]Complete offline capability for field operations
[P]
[P]
[P]What Is Explicitly Out of Scope
[P]Accounting, invoicing, or ERP financial transactions (integration only)
[P]Manufacturing or supply chain management
[P]HR or payroll systems
[P]Customer-facing portals (the platform is internal-only in Phase 1)
[P]
[P]

[P]2. Business Context &amp; Problem Statement
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]2.1  Current State
[P]
[P]The existing Excel-based master client sheet serves as CRM, visit log, revenue ledger, equipment census, and competitive register simultaneously. The key structural problems are:
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]No concurrent editing: Only one person can meaningfully update the file at a time. When multiple reps update separate copies, reconciliation is manual and error-prone.
[P]
[P]
[P]No field capture: Sales representatives visit clients at industrial sites — sugar mills, distilleries, paper plants — in remote areas and record findings on paper, WhatsApp, or personal notes, then update the Excel hours or days later. Data quality degrades and observations are lost.
[P]
[P]
[P]No real-time visibility: Management receives revenue status and visit coverage updates via periodic email reports. There is no live view of what any rep is doing on any given day.
[P]
[P]
[P]No visit-frequency enforcement: Client visit schedules exist as planned entries in a column. There is no system that alerts when a key account is overdue for a visit.
[P]
[P]
[P]No competitive intelligence workflow: The Competitor Data sheet captures installed base at a point in time but is not updated from field visits in any structured way.
[P]
[P]
[P]Reporting is manual: Pivot tables, summaries, and dashboards are built ad hoc and distributed via email. There is no single source of truth that all stakeholders query simultaneously.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]2.2  Desired Future State
[P]
[P]After platform implementation:
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]Every client record is live, structured, and accessible to every authorised user simultaneously from any device.
[P]
[P]
[P]A sales representative arriving at a client site opens the mobile app, checks in via GPS, and completes a structured visit report and equipment assessment form — all on their phone, offline if necessary, syncing automatically when back in range.
[P]
[P]
[P]The moment a rep submits a visit, the client&apos;s record is updated. The zonal manager&apos;s dashboard reflects the visit. Any equipment assessment data updates the competitive share calculation for that client.
[P]
[P]
[P]Management has a live dashboard showing: revenue vs. target by rep and territory, visit frequency compliance by account tier, pipeline by industry segment, and RIL&apos;s competitive market share against every named competitor.
[P]
[P]
[P]No commercial information travels via email or is stored in a spreadsheet.

[P]4.3  Module 3 — Field Data Capture (Mobile)
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]This is the most operationally critical module. It is the mechanism by which every field observation, client interaction, and equipment assessment is captured structured at source — eliminating all post-visit email updates and Excel entries.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]4.3.1  Visit Check-In
[P]
[P]When a rep arrives at a client location:
[P]
[P]
[P]Rep opens the mobile app and taps the planned visit for that client.
[P]
[P]
[P]App requests GPS location. System validates that the rep&apos;s location is within a configurable radius (default: 500 metres) of the client&apos;s registered GPS coordinates.
[P]
[P]
[P]Check-in is recorded with GPS coordinates, timestamp, and user ID.
[P]
[P]
[P]If the rep is outside the expected radius (e.g. visiting a nearby plant not in the system), they can perform a manual check-in with a mandatory location note. This is flagged for manager review.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]FR-3.1.1: GPS check-in is mandatory for all field visits. Remote check-in (without GPS proximity) requires manager override and is flagged in the audit log. This is a data integrity, not a surveillance, control.
[P]
[P]
[P]

[P]4.3.5  Offline Mode — Critical Requirement
[P]
[P]The mobile application shall function fully offline. This is non-negotiable given that client sites are frequently in rural areas with poor or no mobile connectivity.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]All client data required for the day&apos;s planned visits shall be pre-loaded to the device before the rep leaves for the day (auto-sync on Wi-Fi).
[P]
[P]
[P]Visit check-in, visit reports, equipment assessment forms, and contact additions shall all be completable offline with full data capture.

[P]6.1  Architecture Overview
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]The platform shall follow a modern three-tier architecture with a clear separation between the client layer (web and mobile), the API layer, and the data layer.
[P]
[P]
[P]Architecture Layers
[P]PRESENTATION LAYER — Web Application (React.js / Next.js) for desktop/manager use; Progressive Web App (PWA) or React Native for mobile field use.
[P]API LAYER — RESTful API backend (Node.js + Express or Python FastAPI). Handles all business logic, authentication, validation, and real-time sync.
[P]DATA LAYER — PostgreSQL for structured relational data (clients, contacts, visits, revenue); object storage (AWS S3 / Azure Blob) for photos and documents; Redis for caching and real-time push.
[P]OFFLINE LAYER — IndexedDB / SQLite on device for offline data store. Background sync via Service Workers.
[P]INTEGRATION LAYER — API gateway for ERP, Google Maps, and WhatsApp Business API connections.

[P]6.4  Data Migration Plan
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]The existing Excel master sheet (approximately 3,500 client records, 14 sheets) shall be migrated through a structured process:
[P]

[P]
[P]Data audit: Identify and resolve duplicate records, incomplete records, and status inconsistencies in the Excel before migration begins.
[P]
[P]
[P]Field mapping: Map every Excel column to the corresponding structured database field. Document any fields that require transformation (e.g. date format normalisation, capacity bracket standardisation).
[P]
[P]
[P]Validation rules defined: Agree on mandatory field completeness thresholds per record.
[P]
[P]
[P]Staging import: Load data into a staging environment. Generate a validation report showing records that passed, records that require attention, and records that were auto-corrected.
[P]
[P]
[P]Stakeholder review: Business team reviews the staging data and signs off.
[P]
[P]
[P]Production import: Execute the migration. The Excel file is immediately archived as read-only. No new data to be entered in Excel from this point.
[P]
[P]
[P]Post-migration verification: Random sample of 100 records checked by the admin team against the original Excel.

[P]9. Risks &amp; Mitigations
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]Risk
[P]
[P]Likelihood
[P]
[P]Impact
[P]
[P]Mitigation
[P]Field rep adoption resistance — reps revert to WhatsApp/email for visit updates
[P]High
[P]Critical
[P]Involve 2–3 reps as co-designers during field capture module sprint. Ensure app is faster than current process (under 10 min). Manager dashboard makes non-compliance visible, creating natural accountability.
[P]Poor GPS accuracy at industrial sites (remote areas, large plant footprints)
[P]Medium
[P]Medium
[P]Set GPS radius to 500m by default but make it configurable per client. Allow rep to manually attach a satellite image pin for a client if the registered GPS is inaccurate.
[P]Data migration quality — Excel has inconsistencies, duplicates, and missing data
[P]High
[P]High
[P]Run data audit before migration. Allocate 2 weeks specifically for data cleaning with business team. Stage import with validation report and business sign-off before production push.
[P]Offline sync conflicts — two people update the same record while one is offline
[P]Low
[P]Medium
[P]Last-write-wins for non-conflicting fields. Conflict flagging for critical fields (status, revenue) with mandatory admin resolution. Design minimises shared field editing.
[P]ERP integration delay — ERP API not ready within project timeline
[P]Medium
[P]Low
[P]Revenue manual entry is the fallback and is fully functional. ERP integration is Phase 4 and does not block earlier phases.
[P]Connectivity worse than anticipated — sync takes too long or fails
[P]Medium
[P]High
[P]Robust local queue with retry logic. Sync status always visible to rep. Background sync does not block the UI. Test in genuine rural connectivity conditions during Phase 2.
[P]Scope creep — requests to add features mid-development
[P]High
[P]Medium
[P]Strict change control process from Phase 2 onwards. New features logged in a backlog, reviewed and prioritised quarterly. Phase boundary reviews with stakeholders.

[P]Appendix C — Glossary
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]Term
[P]
[P]Definition
[P]PCP
[P]Progressive Cavity Pump — a type of positive displacement pump; the primary RIL product category
[P]MMP
[P]Multistage Mixed-flow Pump — the secondary RIL product category
[P]TCD
[P]Tonnes of Cane Crushed per Day — the capacity measure for sugar mills
[P]KLPD
[P]Kilolitres Per Day — the capacity measure for distilleries
[P]OEM
[P]Original Equipment Manufacturer — a client who integrates RIL pumps into their own equipment or systems
[P]End User
[P]A client who uses RIL pumps directly in their own plant operations
[P]Group Mill
[P]A client that is one of multiple plants under the same parent company / ownership group
[P]Merchant Exporter
[P]A client who buys RIL products for re-export to overseas buyers
[P]Tour Route / Beat
[P]A named geographic cluster of clients assigned to a rep for regular visit coverage
[P]Installed Base
[P]The total number of pumps (of any brand) physically installed and operating at a client&apos;s facility
[P]Displacement Opportunity
[P]A competitor pump that is end-of-life or poorly performing, representing a potential RIL replacement sale
[P]At-Risk Account
[P]An active client with no orders in 18+ months and no open pipeline — flagged for immediate rep action
[P]FY / Financial Year
[P]Indian financial year: April 1 to March 31 (e.g. FY 25-26 = April 2025 to March 2026)
